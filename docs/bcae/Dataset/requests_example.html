<p><b>Theuderic IV</b> (<abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;">&#8201;712</span>  737,  <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <i>Thierry</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was the <a href="/wiki/Merovingian" class="mw-redirect" title="Merovingian">Merovingian</a> <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Franks" class="mw-redirect" title="King of the Franks">king of the Franks</a> from 721 until his death in 737. He was the son of  <a href="/wiki/Dagobert_III" title="Dagobert III">Dagobert III</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERosenwein200984_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERosenwein200984-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During his reign, his realm was controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_the_palace" title="Mayor of the palace">mayor of the palace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a>. The king mostly stayed at <a href="/wiki/Chelles_Abbey" title="Chelles Abbey">Chelles Abbey</a>, then in <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau-Thierry" title="Chteau-Thierry">Chteau-Thierry</a>.
</p><p>After his death, the Frankish throne remained vacant for seven years, until <a href="/wiki/Carloman_(mayor_of_the_palace)" title="Carloman (mayor of the palace)">Carloman</a>, one of Charles Martel's sons and successors, arranged for <a href="/wiki/Childeric_III" title="Childeric III">Childeric III</a>, the last Merovingian king, to succeed him. Theuderic IV may have been the father of Childeric III, but this remains uncertain.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERosenwein200984_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERosenwein200984-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
